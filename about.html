Elinor Wilder lives on a quiet mountain where the trees are tall, the wind is sharp, and the sea is always somewhere far below.

She writes heartfelt shifter romance with neurodivergent leads, tortured/wounded alphas, and character driven arcs.

Her Claw Ridge Series follows wildland firefighter shifters in the Teton Mountains who find their mates among women who've never fit conventional molds – from Wall Street burnouts to autistic veterinarians.

She focuses on authentic representation, sensory-rich storytelling, and bonds that form through emotional safety rather than instalust.

In her books you will find found family, protector alphas, and love stories where being different becomes the reason you're chosen.

She shares her cabin with two cats and an abundance of wildlife that treat her property like their personal highway.

When she's not writing heart-pounding wilderness romances with impossible rescues and competent heroes, she's probably outside, pretending she's not waiting for wolves.
