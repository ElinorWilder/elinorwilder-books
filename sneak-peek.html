Crouching down, I pulled open the top drawer under the kitchen countertop in Emma’s tiny kitchen. Scout immediately shoved his entire head in there, making me chuckle in exasperation.

“Bud, there’s creamer and coffee pods, not bones.” He didn’t care. I left him to root around while I opened another one. Hazelnut creamer again. Damn. With a sinking heart, I opened the last one. It was full of the same.

I blew a breath to calm myself down. It wasn’t a big deal. Even though I hated the stuff, I could tolerate it for one morning. I was a big boy, a fucking alpha. Grumbling under my breath, I started the coffee maker and waited for the cabin to fill with that cloying smell. The memory came back from our student days as if it was only yesterday.

Clearly, I needed to go shopping. Preferably this very afternoon. Better to check if Emma had anything suitable for cooking, or were it all her granola-cardboard-bars boxes? Scout diligently followed me around as I explored, opening and inspecting every nook and cranny of the kitchen, that was basically just a wall with cabinets and a cooktop range with a single burner. I loved the minimalist setup. I did. Just the hazelnut creamer was about to get on my nerves.

Why was I surprised though? Emma had always loved that shit. It was that thought that made me realize the coffee had finished brewing and yet I was fine. How had I forgotten that I could no longer smell her annoying flavor? And why was I not happy about it? I didn’t have to be annoyed anymore. But the pain that hit me was a shock. I could no longer smell it. My brain was replaying a memory that felt like I could only see in black and white. The color version was gone.

“Connor?” Emma’s sleepy voice snapped me back to the present.
